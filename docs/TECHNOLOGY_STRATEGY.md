# Technology Strategy

Business OS should be simple for customers and maintainable for engineers.

## Current Technology Direction

- Flutter for cross-platform delivery.
- Offline-first workflows where local reliability matters.
- Cloud-native direction for future shared services.
- Shared Core for common platform capabilities.
- Reusable business logic.
- Configurable rules.
- Automated testing.
- Documentation-driven governance.
- Architecture-first product development.

## Why These Choices Matter

Flutter supports a consistent product experience across Android, iOS, Windows,
and Web. This is important for small businesses that may use different devices
inside the same operation.

Offline-first design protects daily workflows when connectivity is unreliable
or unavailable.

Shared Core and reusable business logic reduce duplication as new Assistants
are added.

Configurable rules help Business OS support different businesses without
hardcoding company-specific assumptions.

Testing and documentation make the platform easier to maintain over time.

## Long-Term Scalability

Business OS should scale by adding focused Assistants, not by building a
monolith. Shared interfaces, clear boundaries, and reusable services are more
important than short-term speed.

# Market Opportunity

Millions of small businesses still operate with fragmented tools.

Many use paper. Many use spreadsheets. Many use chat messages and disconnected
apps. These tools are familiar and inexpensive, but they often create duplicated
work, missing records, and uncertainty when the owner needs to make decisions.

Traditional ERP is not always a realistic alternative. It can be too expensive,
too broad, and too difficult for owner-operated businesses or teams with fewer
than 30 employees.

Business OS focuses on the gap between fragile manual operations and heavy
enterprise systems.

## Customer Context

Business OS is designed for practical businesses:

- Restaurants
- Coffee shops
- Street food vendors
- Grocery stores
- Beauty salons
- Car repair shops
- Laundry shops
- Bars
- Local retailers
- Small warehouses
- Family businesses

These customers need clarity more than complexity.

## Opportunity

The opportunity is not only to digitize records. It is to make operations more
understandable:

- Attendance that becomes payroll-ready records.
- Sales that become daily visibility.
- Inventory that becomes early warnings.
- Taxes that become preparation instead of fear.
- AI that explains instead of impresses.

No fabricated market-size numbers are included here. The opportunity should be
validated through customer conversations, usage, retention, and willingness to
pay.
